# CalTrack FAQ

## What is CalTrack?
CalTrack is a simple, local-first nutrition tracker focused on calories, protein, and key micronutrients.

## Do I need an account?
No. CalTrack v1 does **not** require an account.

## Where is my data stored?
Your entries are stored **on your device**. If you uninstall the app, your data may be lost.

## Is CalTrack medical advice?
No. CalTrack is for informational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice.

## How does barcode scanning work?
When you scan a barcode, CalTrack looks up nutrition details using public food databases. Some products may not be found or may have incomplete data.

## Why are some nutrition values missing?
Food databases can be incomplete. For some foods, calories or micronutrients may be missing or provided per 100g rather than per serving.

## What does “Servings” do?
Servings multiplies the nutrition values. For example, 2 servings doubles calories, protein, and other nutrients.

## How do Favorites work?
- Long-press a Recent item to add it to Favorites.
- Tap a Favorite to log it again quickly.

## How do reminders work?
Reminders are local notifications on your device. You can enable or disable them in Settings.

## Can I share the app with friends?
If you’re running Expo Go via tunnel, you can share the Expo link. For a permanent share link, the app needs to be published (EAS Update) or released in the app store.

## How accurate is the nutrition data?
It can be wrong. Always verify labels and serving sizes, especially for health conditions, allergies, or strict diets.
